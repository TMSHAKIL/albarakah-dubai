Why Al Barakah is the Best Biryani in Dubai

Dubai has thousands of restaurants serving biryani. So what makes Al Barakah's biryani stand out from the rest? The answer is simple — we never compromise on the process.

Our Kacchi Biryani starts the night before. The mutton is marinated for a minimum of 12 hours in a blend of yoghurt, ginger, garlic, and imported Bangladeshi spices. The next morning, it is placed in a heavy-bottomed pot with aged Basmati rice, sealed with dough, and slow-cooked on a low flame for over four hours. The result is meat so tender it falls off the bone and rice that has absorbed every flavour of the marinade.

Fresh Biryani Every Day — No Reheating

We cook our biryani fresh every day. We never reheat biryani from the previous day. This is our non-negotiable standard and the reason our guests keep coming back.

What is the difference between Kacchi Biryani and regular biryani?

In Kacchi Biryani, raw marinated meat is cooked together with the rice inside a sealed pot. In regular biryani, the meat and rice are cooked separately and then layered. Kacchi takes longer but produces much more flavourful, tender results.
