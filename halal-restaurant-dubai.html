A Truly Halal Restaurant in Dubai

When Muslim families and visitors search for halal food in Dubai, they want more than just a restaurant that claims to be halal. They want genuine assurance — verified certification, transparent sourcing, and a dining environment that reflects their values.

At Al Barakah, halal is not a marketing claim. It is a foundation. Our halal certificate from Dubai Municipality is displayed at the entrance. Every supplier we use is on Dubai's approved halal supplier list. Our kitchen has never and will never serve alcohol or non-halal meat.

Halal Dining for Muslim Families in Dubai

Al Barakah welcomes Muslim families, tourists from across the Muslim world, and expats from Bangladesh, Pakistan, India, and the wider South Asian community. Our private family sections provide a comfortable, modest dining environment for families with children.

Best Halal Restaurant Near Dubai Airport

Deira is one of the closest areas to Dubai International Airport. If you are transiting through Dubai and looking for a halal meal, Al Barakah on Al Rigga Road is just 10 minutes from Terminal 1 and Terminal 3.

FAQ

Halal Questions

Is Al Barakah Restaurant halal certified in Dubai?

Yes. Al Barakah Restaurant holds a valid halal certificate from Dubai Municipality. All our meat is sourced from approved halal suppliers and the certificate is displayed at our entrance.

Do you serve alcohol at Al Barakah?

No. Al Barakah is a 100% alcohol-free restaurant. We serve fresh juices, soft drinks, and traditional South Asian beverages only.

Is all the meat at Al Barakah halal?

Yes. Every piece of meat we serve — chicken, beef, mutton, and fish — is sourced from halal-certified suppliers approved by Dubai Municipality. We do not use any non-halal meat.

Is Al Barakah suitable for families during Ramadan?

Yes. We offer Iftar sets during Ramadan and welcome families for Suhoor. Our restaurant provides a respectful, family-friendly environment throughout the holy month.
